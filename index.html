Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

The higher the amount of poverty in a state, the higher the risk that people don't have healthcare. States like Texas and Georgia stand out with an above 20% lack in healthcare.

People with lower income tend to be more obese. This is more than likely because cheaper food such as fast food is readily available, cheaper and easier to come by compared to grocery stores, such as WholeFoods.

People with lower income tend to be heavier smokers. Especially. people who make around $40k. People who make $70k+ are the most health conscious.
